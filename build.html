BUILDS

The following builds are listed with no particular order in mind aside from their main damage source. In terms of damage output, right now Ice builds are at the top, followed by Bow builds.
Difficulty denotes how hard a build to use it effectively, while Power denotes the DPS ceiling in respect to other builds.
A Light Blue Souljade names means they can be acquired on Normal and above difficulty.
A Purple Souljade names means they can be acquired on Hard and above difficulty.
A Red Souljade names means they can be acquired on Nightscream difficulty.

Description

This extremely easy-to-use build features no penalties and is recommended for most players to start with. Your attacks will build up Frostbite on enemies, which will explode into an Ice Blast once certain thresholds are met. Each Ice Blast will summon 3 Lotus (up to 4 per second) that will surround you and explode for high damage when they expire or when you exceed 4 total.

Over time, as you activate more Ice Blasts, you will charge up an extremely strong attack known as Chain Crush. You will see a symbol appear in the top left that indicates it is ready to be used. To activate it, use any uncharged melee attack.

[Moderation] +100% allows you to use your Hero Skill after every Ice Blast.

Description

Your attacks will build up Frostbite on enemies, which will explode into an Ice Blast once certain thresholds are met. Each Ice Blast will generate a certain amount of Frostbite on yourself, which will attempt to freeze you after 2 Ice Blasts.

You can defuse this freeze attempt or use any ability that applies golden focus to yourself, such as Justina's Skill, Shadowswipe. After reaching maximum Frostbite, Frostbite Surge will activate, preventing you from being frozen for 25 seconds and allowing you to summon Ice Thorns whenever you use your Hero's Skill (this has a 4-second cooldown).

After dealing enough Ice damage, you will charge up an extremely strong attack known as Chain Crush. You will see a symbol appear in the top left that indicates it is ready to be used. To activate it, use any uncharged melee attack.

[Moderation] +100% is necessary to use this build effectively.

Placeholder Attributes

Poison Blast DMG & Melee Weapon DMG.

Description

Poison Blasts activate Noxious Blast, which applies 1 stack of [Toxified] to the enemy, and Pestilent Pool, which generates a poison pool that applies up to 2 [Toxified] stacks to enemies standing within it.

Standing in the pool for 2 seconds inflicts [Toxified] on yourself, allowing you to transfer 2 [Toxified] stacks to the enemy by using a Martial Skill or the 3rd attack in any uncharged attack combo (excluding Greatsword and Polesword) via Infectious Touch.

Furthermore, this will activate Toxin Surge, which prevents you from being poisoned for 15 seconds and allows you to apply an additional stack of [Toxified] whenever you use a Martial Skill.

When an enemy reaches 6 stacks of [Toxified], Toxin Blast activates, dealing major damage to the surrounding area. When you have applied [Toxified] to enemies 11 times, Chain Crush will be ready, adding a massive damaging attack to your next uncharged melee attack.

[Moderation] +75% or higher recommended if pursuing a Mod version of this build.

Recommended Heroes

Wuchen & Kurumi

Description

A slow starting build that requires you to activate Deadeye by hitting your shots on enemies. Counterstrikes give significantly more Deadeye charge (almost 3x the amount) and headshots give slightly more (20% more), so good aim is heavily rewarded. Headshots will deal greatly increased damage with this build, so it is heavily advised to aim for the head. When shooting a weakpoint or head while in the air and Deadeye is active, you will backflip and hover in the air for a short period and can fire a powerful shot. This will make it easier to hit headshots from safety, with the angle you backflip dependent on the angle you hit the enemy. Not recommended for console players due to the difficulty of aiming.
